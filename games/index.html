Thesis

Prioritize, Produce, Playtest: Iterative Audio Systems Design in Modern Video Games.

For my MFA thesis, I practiced and wrote about my discipline: the job of the Technical Sound Designer. To do so I expanded on Unity's 2018 FPS Sample project, re-implementing the audio via FMOD Studio and expanding further on certain systems using C#.

Technically, it was a challenge, as it was built with a home-grown ECS implementation before Unity properly supported it the following year.Ultimately, I was able to make it work. In terms of art and theory, it helped give structure to the approach I use professionally today.

Roboxing

Arcade-style, 1v1 Robot boxing.

This project was made with my friend Tyler Eichten in collaboration with Husky Game Development at Michigan Tech University. Originally built for an arcade cabinet, we later adapted it to work with Xbox-style controllers. The game was written in Unity, integrated with FMOD Studio, and used Github for version control.

The full soundtrack is available for free from Bandcamp (embedded), and the game is available for free below.

Triseum & LIVE Lab

Games for education and research.

In November 2016, I was contracted to do music composition work for Triseum LTD, a developer in Bryan, TX known for their Arté and Variant higher-level educational game series. From January through August 2017, I was also involved with related projects with the LIVE Lab at Texas A&M, an educational research lab focused on game development.

Student Games

The House Where They Dwell

The horror was supposed to be staged.

The House Where They Dwell is a first-person cooperative game from Half-Brain Games where you and your ghost-hunting TV show crew escape the house, built in Unreal Engine 4 using the native audio engine and Blueprints, and version controlled with Git. I was the Audio Director and audio dev, working with a team of 5 to produce and implement the score, VO, sound effects, and audio vision.

Some of the largest challenges we faced were with sheer audio scope, with the variety of enemies, items, and physics interactions (let alone split-screen audio). I'm most proud of the physics audio, where we covered it, and the VO.

Due to artistic choices with the final boss being a topless spider witch, the game was listed as "Adults Only," despite no other suggestions of adult content. It's jank and buggy (pun not intended) but the vibes are immaculate.

Sumra

Spring will come again.

Sumra is a first-person atmospheric puzzle game that my good friend Brittany Johnson and I were "outsourced" for. It was developed completely in Unreal Engine 4, which led to plenty of fun with Blueprints and the native audio engine, and version controlled with Perforce. My primary focus was environment and item interactions, as well as general implementation and rough attempts to work with linear in-engine cinematics. For our efforts, we won an award for Best Game Sound at SCAD's 2020 Entelechy Awards.

You can view the project's website here for more information.

FooFarYall

Local Competitive minigames.

A silly little Western game, made for Chillennium Game Jam 2016, using Unity and Fungus, played on one keyboard. All music (and some sound effects) were recorded on-site during that time. For our efforts we won the award for Special Recognition in Sound; this project directly led to me pursuing Game Audio as a viable career, and to my involvement with Triseum & the LIVE Lab.

You can find both tracks on my Bandcamp album of miscellaneous tracks, and try the game with a friend if you like.
